Research Interests

I focus on how primates deploy prospective memory, cognitive flexibility, and attention restoration when navigating complex tasks. My goal is to translate laboratory findings into insights about human learning, motivation, and mental wellness.

  • Understanding the trade-offs between prospective and retrospective working-memory strategies.
  • Designing translational cognitive tasks for rhesus macaques that inform human learning.
  • Linking environmental factors and break quality to attention restoration and motivation.
