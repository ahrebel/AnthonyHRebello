Comparative Cognition

Memory Strategies in Rhesus Macaques

Laboratory of Comparative Primate Cognition | May 2024 - Present
R Python Experimental Design

I am conducting an independent research project examining how rhesus macaques adapt working memory strategies to task demands. The study focuses on the distinction between prospective (planning for future action) and retrospective (remembering past events) memory codes.

  • Developed a "many-to-one" and "match-to-sample" paradigm to isolate specific memory strategies.
  • Personally trained rhesus macaques on touchscreen tasks and managed daily data collection.
  • Utilized Python for data and statistical analysis.

Environmental Psychology

Break Quality & Attentional Restoration

Emory Dept. of Psychology | 2024
SPSS Data Analysis

Investigated how break quality and length impact attentional restoration. We hypothesized that break quality (e.g., nature exposure vs. phone use) would moderate restoration effects.

Rebello, A., Cordish, B., Womack, J., & Piramal, N. (2024). How break quality and length impact attentional restoration. Poster presented at Psychology Department Research Symposium, Emory University.
